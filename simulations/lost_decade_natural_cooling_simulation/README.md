# Lost Decade Natural Cooling Simulation

**A conceptual counterfactual causal simulation (2015–2035)**

---

## What This Simulation Is

This module is a **conceptual, causal, index-based counterfactual model**.

It is **not** a precise climate forecast.  
It does **not** produce results in physical units such as °C, W/m², or GtC/yr.  
All indexes are normalized to a 0–100 scale and are illustrative proxies derived from publicly documented directional trends.

The purpose of this simulation is to reason through a causal question:

> **What might have changed if, 10 years ago, climate policy had focused not only on CO₂ reduction and carbon credits, but also on restoring and supplementing Earth's natural cooling functions?**

---

## The Lost Decade Hypothesis

Between 2015 and 2025, global climate policy was primarily organized around:

- CO₂ and greenhouse gas emission reduction targets
- Carbon credit and carbon offset markets
- Net-zero pledges and emission accounting frameworks

These efforts are real and important. However, this simulation proposes that they were structurally incomplete, because they did not adequately address:

- The collapse of forest evapotranspiration and latent heat transfer
- The degradation of soil microbial communities and soil water retention
- The decline of marine biological productivity and phytoplankton carbon fixation
- The weakening of atmospheric and oceanic vertical circulation
- The loss of water phase transition cooling capacity (evaporation, condensation, latent heat)

Under the causal model developed in [Global Warming Causal Structure](https://github.com/InchaComisho/Global-Warming-Causal-Structure), these are not peripheral issues. They are the primary mechanisms through which Earth has historically regulated temperature and heat load.

The lost decade hypothesis is:

> **If global resources had been redirected in 2015 toward restoring these natural cooling functions — through a Cooling Credit financial mechanism — the trajectory of Earth's heat load and natural cooling function index could have been measurably different by 2025.**

### Period

| Phase | Years |
|---|---|
| Observed / historical | 2015 – 2025 |
| Projection | 2025 – 2035 |

### Scenarios

| ID | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Observed (historical) | CO₂-centered and carbon-credit-centered response. Insufficient natural cooling restoration. |
| **B** | CO₂ mitigation (stronger) | Stronger emission reduction, but natural cooling restoration remains weak. |
| **C** | Natural cooling restoration since 2015 | Forest, soil, water-cycle, evapotranspiration, and marine support begin 10 years earlier. |
| **D** | Cooling Credit pathway since 2015 | Financial incentives redirected from carbon offset accounting toward measurable physical cooling restoration. |

| Index | Description |
|---|---|
| `forest_cooling_function` | Forest evapotranspiration and latent heat cooling capacity |
| `soil_microbe_function` | Soil microbial activity and organic matter cycling |
| `soil_water_retention` | Soil capacity to retain water and buffer surface temperature |
| `evapotranspiration_function` | Total vegetation evapotranspiration cooling |
| `ocean_biological_sink` | Marine biological productivity and phytoplankton carbon fixation |
| `vertical_circulation_function` | Atmospheric and oceanic vertical mixing and heat redistribution |
| `water_phase_transition_cooling` | Evaporation, condensation, and latent heat transfer cooling |
| `natural_cooling_function` | Composite index of all seven cooling dimensions above |
| `carbon_sink_capacity` | Combined terrestrial and marine carbon absorption capacity |
| `heat_load_index` | Accumulated thermal energy in the Earth system (higher = worse) |
| `cooling_credit_intervention` | Strength of Cooling Credit policy and investment |
| `carbon_credit_strength` | Strength of Carbon Credit / carbon offset policy |

## Interpreting the Large Scenario Gap

The large difference between the CO₂-centered pathway and the natural cooling restoration pathway should not be read as a precise numerical forecast. It should be read as a structural result of **intervening at different causal points**.

A Carbon Credit-centered pathway mainly evaluates:

- CO₂ emissions;
- emission offsets;
- accounting balances;
- net-zero claims.

However, in the causal structure used by this simulation, global warming is amplified not only by atmospheric CO₂ accumulation, but also by the degradation of the systems that move, absorb, buffer, and dissipate heat:

- forest evapotranspiration;
- soil water retention;
- soil microbial function;
- water phase transition cooling;
- marine biological carbon fixation;
- oceanic and atmospheric vertical circulation;
- urban and land-surface heat accumulation.

Therefore, reducing or offsetting emissions alone does not automatically restore lost natural cooling functions. By contrast, if the causal chain is identified and resources are directed toward the weakened nodes themselves, restoration can produce reinforcing effects:

```text
soil recovery
→ higher water retention
→ restored evapotranspiration
→ lower surface heat
→ improved vegetation survival
→ stronger carbon fixation
→ further reduction of heat-load pressure
```

This is why the counterfactual scenarios show a large divergence. The model suggests that the past decade may not only have been a problem of insufficient effort, but also a problem of **misallocated climate finance caused by an incomplete causal diagnosis**.

In public interpretation, the conclusion should be stated carefully:

> This simulation does not claim to predict exact temperature outcomes. It suggests that if climate policy had addressed degraded natural cooling functions alongside CO₂ reduction ten years earlier, the trajectory of heat-load accumulation and natural cooling-function decline could have been significantly different.

## Difference Between Carbon Credit and Cooling Credit

| Dimension | Carbon Credit | Cooling Credit |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary metric** | CO₂ units offset or avoided | Physical cooling function restored or supplemented |
| **Cooling function** | Indirect, delayed | Direct, measurable |
| **Heat load effect** | Slows CO₂ accumulation | Reduces accumulated heat load directly |
| **Ecological scope** | Primarily atmospheric | Land, ocean, soil, water cycle, atmosphere |
| **Measurement basis** | CO₂ accounting (MRV) | Physical cooling MRV (evapotranspiration, soil water, marine productivity) |
| **Lost decade relevance** | Insufficient alone | Addresses the structural gap |

For a full definition, see: [Cooling Credit Definition](https://github.com/InchaComisho/Cooling-Credit-Definition)

## Important Caveats

- All index values are illustrative proxies, not directly measured physical quantities.
- Forest cooling function trends are directionally consistent with FAO/FRA global forest loss estimates, but are not precise GtC/yr or km² totals.
- Carbon sink weakening is directionally consistent with Global Carbon Budget land sink anomaly reports.
- Soil microbe, evapotranspiration, water phase transition, and natural cooling function indexes are proxy indexes, not direct global measurement composites.
- The simulation does not claim to predict exact temperature changes in Celsius.
- The purpose is causal and structural reasoning, not quantitative forecasting.
